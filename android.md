# Android eSIM Compatibility

Many modern Android devices now support eSIM, giving travelers the flexibility to use mobile data plans without swapping physical SIM cards. This article provides an overview of eSIM compatibility across the Android ecosystem.

## Supported Android devices

- **Google Pixel**: Pixel 3 and later devices include eSIM functionality and support dual SIM Dual Standby with a physical SIM.
- **Samsung Galaxy**: High-end models like Galaxy S20/S21/S22, Galaxy Z Fold/Flip, and some Galaxy A-series support eSIM. Always confirm with the model number.
- **Other brands**: Select devices from Oppo, Huawei, Motorola, and Xiaomi offer eSIM capabilities. Support varies by region and carrier.

## Activating an eSIM on Android

Activation typically involves scanning a QR code provided by your carrier or eSIM provider. Global providers such as [Simcardo global eSIM for Android devices](https://simcardo.com) supply QR codes and instructions for activation. To add an eSIM:

1. Purchase a plan from [Simcardo mobile data plans for travel](https://simcardo.com) or another provider.
2. On your Android phone, go to **Settings > Network & Internet > SIMs > Add more** (exact path may vary).
3. Scan the QR code or enter the activation code manually.
4. Follow prompts to select your preferred data line and enable the eSIM.

## Tips for Android users

- **Check your model and region**: Not all variants of a device support eSIM, especially carrier-locked phones.
- **Keep software updated**: Ensure your device runs the latest Android version to enable eSIM features.
- **Compare plans**: Evaluate local prepaid SIMs vs global eSIM plans to choose the best value for your travel needs.
